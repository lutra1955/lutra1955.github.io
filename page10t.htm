Ruwer.

At the end of September we had to go to Trier. A bit of research on the maps and on the internet focussed my attention to the river Ruwer.
The hotel that appeared on the screen was the Karlsmühle. An old mill, nicely situated near the river and between the vineyards.
As the answer on my e-mailed request for information if it was possible to get a permit to fish the Ruwer when one stayed in the hotel, I was directed to contact Mr Peter Geiber, the owner of the winehouse Karlsmühle and also the owner of the fishing rights of that stretch of river. When I called Peter, he told me that getting a permit wouldn't be a problem. And so I stood on his doorstep a few weeks later. I alse used the occasion to buy, apart from the permit, two bottles of 2005 Lorenzhöfer Mäuerchen Riesling Kabinett. When I got home, it turned out to be a delicious wine and I felt sorry that I did not bring a few more bottles home.
Tuesday, after work, I could go fishing for just an hour or two. A bit away from the hotel I entered the river and within fifteen minutes the first fish was caught. A beautifully marked brown trout. The Ruwer is a very nice stream, not too wide, and with the dead summer low level it was very easy to wade. From time to time I got a fish and so I was having a great time. The landscape in the Ruwer valley is beautiful, with the forests and the vineyards on the hills. A while later a grayling came to the net, it was appreciated for I had not got one in a long time.
You used to get a lot of grayling in the river Kyll, years ago. But that has all changed these days. The kingfisher showed itself a few times, what a magnificent bird.
On Wednesday there was also the opportunity to go fishing a few hours and again a few trout and grayling were caught. After the fishing we stayed to have dinner at the hotel terrace. The trout tasted delicious, even despite that I did not catch it myself. On the Wednesday there often are life music performances. This evening a band played very good blues. All in all a very good place to stay for a while.
